# Skill: The Interrogation Method

**Source:** "5 Claude Skills That Make You Unstoppable At Work"
**Use when:** Starting any deliverable that needs to be tailored to a specific audience, company, or situation — strategy documents, client proposals, board presentations, performance reviews, or any output where you'd normally end up rewriting Claude's first draft.

## The Problem

The typical approach is to open Claude and type something like "Write me a marketing strategy." Claude immediately produces a couple of pages of generic recommendations that could apply to any company in any industry. The output is technically correct but useless, because Claude had zero context about your audience, your budget, your competitive landscape, what you've already tried, or what your CEO actually cares about seeing in the document.

Claude defaults to being immediately helpful, which means it fills every gap in your prompt with assumptions. The quality gap between assumed context and provided context is the difference between a draft you delete and a draft you send.

## The Instruction

Before Claude writes anything, tell it to interview you first:

> "Interview me about every aspect of this project before you produce anything. Ask one question at a time until you fully understand what I need."

## What Happens

Claude asks targeted, non-surface-level questions — not "what's your industry," but things like:
- How has the target audience shifted since last quarter?
- What worked last quarter and what didn't?
- What are the budget constraints — optimizing for growth or efficiency?
- Who is the deliverable actually for, and what format do they expect?
- What does success look like in specific, measurable terms?

By the time Claude has asked 10–15 questions, it has more context than most team members get in a briefing meeting. A 12-question interrogation can take a client proposal that normally needs three drafts down to one that lands on the first try.

## Why It Works

The interrogation forces Claude to ask instead of assume. Once it has the full picture, the recommendations are specific to your company, the metrics reference your actual targets, and the language matches the tone your leadership expects.

## When to Use It

Anytime you find yourself rewriting Claude's output to fit your specific situation, an interrogation up front would have prevented that rewrite. Best for: strategy documents, client proposals, board presentations, performance reviews, and any email or document where tone and specificity matter.
