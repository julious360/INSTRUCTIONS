# Skill: The Perspective Shift

**Source:** "5 Claude Skills That Make You Unstoppable At Work"
**Use when:** Reviewing any finished deliverable before it goes out — reports, proposals, presentations — to catch problems before the actual audience does.

## The Problem

The natural instinct is to read through Claude's output, make a few edits, and send it. The problem is you're reviewing it from one angle — your own. The people actually receiving it read it from completely different angles: your CFO reads for financial rigor, your client reads for relevance to their specific situation, your board reads for strategic risk. A single review from your perspective misses what each of them would catch instantly.

## The Instruction

After Claude produces any deliverable, run multiple perspective reviews before accepting it. Each pass takes about 30 seconds to run and 2 minutes to read.

**Pass 1 — Financial / internal rigor:**
> "Review this as my CFO would. What financial gaps, unsupported claims, or missing ROI calculations would they flag?"

This catches things like vague cost projections, missing ROI timelines, unquantified risk, and benefit claims that aren't tied to specific numbers — the exact things a CFO would circle in red.

**Pass 2 — The actual audience:**
> "Now review this as our biggest client would. What feels generic? What doesn't address their specific situation? What would make them feel like this was written for someone else?"

This catches a completely different set of issues: sections where examples don't match the client's industry, language that's too internal, recommendations that feel templated instead of tailored.

**Pass 3 — Skeptical oversight:**
> "Review this as a skeptical board member. What risks aren't addressed? What assumptions haven't been validated? Where are the logical gaps?"

This surfaces unaddressed risks, unvalidated assumptions, timelines that don't account for real-world delays (e.g., legal review), and positioning claims a client could easily challenge.

## Why It Works

Each perspective catches a different category of problem — the CFO pass catches numbers, the client pass catches relevance and tone, the board pass catches risk and logic. The full three-perspective review adds less than 10 minutes to your workflow, but the output that survives all three is significantly stronger than anything a single review produces.

## Customizing the Perspective

Build the reviewer around whoever is actually receiving the work:
- Presenting to a technical team → review from their CTO's perspective
- Pitching investors → review from a skeptical LP's perspective

The more specific you make the reviewer, the more useful the feedback becomes.
