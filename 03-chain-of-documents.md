# Skill: Chain of Documents

**Source:** "5 Claude Skills That Make You Unstoppable At Work"
**Use when:** Building any deliverable longer than a single page — proposals, strategy documents, board presentations, market analyses, competitive intelligence reports — where depth and structure matter more than speed.

## The Problem

The default approach is to try to get Claude to produce a full document in one prompt: "Write me a business proposal for expanding into the European market." Claude produces something that reads like a first draft, because it is one — a single pass from a model trying to cover research, structure, tone, and detail all at once.

## The Instruction

Break the deliverable into four phases. Each phase builds on the last and gets validated before the next one begins.

**Phase 1 — The brief:**
> "I need a business proposal for [X]. Before we write anything, draft a one-page brief covering the objective, the audience, the key questions we need to answer, and the structure of the final document."

This brief becomes the blueprint for everything that follows. This is where you catch problems early — if the brief targets the wrong audience or asks the wrong questions, you fix it in 2 minutes instead of rewriting a 15-page document later.

**Phase 2 — Research:**
> "Based on this brief, research the key questions we identified. For each one, give me the data points, the risks, and the opportunities."

You get structured research mapped to each question in the brief. If web search is enabled, it pulls current data; if internal documents are in the conversation, it references those too.

**Phase 3 — The outline:**
> "Using the brief and the research, draft a detailed section-by-section outline with the key argument for each section and the supporting evidence from the research phase."

The outline is where the structure of the final document takes shape. Reviewing an outline is roughly 10x faster than reviewing a full draft — if a section is misplaced or an argument doesn't flow, you catch it here.

**Phase 4 — The full draft:**
Claude writes the complete document with the brief, the research, and the outline all in context.

## Why It Works

The document that comes out of four validated phases reads like something a team produced over a week. The arguments build on each other, the data supports the claims, and the structure flows logically — because each phase was checked before the next one started. The phases add roughly 20 minutes to the total process, but the output quality is in a different league from one-shot prompting.

## When to Use It

Proposals, strategy documents, board presentations, market analyses, competitive intelligence reports, and anything else where depth and structure matter more than speed.
